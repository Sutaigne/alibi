================================================================
  ALIBI - START HERE
================================================================

Someone asked you to prove your gaming setup is clean? That is
what this folder is for. You run one scan, four report files
appear on your Desktop, and you send them to whoever asked.
Nothing gets installed, nothing on your PC gets changed, and
nothing about your PC is sent anywhere.


HOW TO USE
==========

  1. Right-click "Run scan.bat"
  2. Pick "Run as administrator"
  3. Click YES on the UAC prompt
  4. Wait about 2-3 minutes (scans both PC and console-rig sides)
  5. Check your Desktop for the four output files:
       AlibiReport_<timestamp>.txt           the PC report
       AlibiReport_<timestamp>_visual.html   visual of the PC report
       AlibiRigReport_<timestamp>.txt           the console-rig report
       AlibiRigReport_<timestamp>_visual.html   visual of the console report
  6. Send the .txt files (or the .html visuals - both work) to
     whoever asked.


WHY TWO SCANS?
==============

One scan looks at your PC as a PC gamer's machine (PC cheats, DMA
artifacts, HWID spoofers, etc.). The other scan looks at it as a
console-rig PC (capture-card software, vision aimbots, input-
adapter configurators like XIM/Cronus/ReaSnow). Running both
covers every scenario this PC could be involved in.

If you only play on PC, the console-rig report will be CLEAN -
that's expected. If you only play on console with a PC in the
rig, the PC report will be CLEAN. Either way, the relevant report
is the one with findings.


REQUIREMENTS
============

  - Windows 10 or 11.
  - You must run as administrator (UAC prompt). Without admin,
    the scan loses access to several forensic sources (Prefetch,
    BAM, USB history, driver-signing flags, ShimCache, full
    process module list). Half the report would say "Access
    denied" - the scan won't run without admin.
  - About 1 MB of free disk space for the reports.
  - No internet connection needed for the core scan. One opt-in
    network call (the LOLDrivers BYOVD cross-reference at
    loldrivers.io) prompts you with Y/N - press anything other
    than Y to skip. The scan runs identically either way.


SAFETY IN ONE LINE
==================

Every scan is read-only. Nothing leaves your machine. All scripts
in the "alibi-engine\scanner" subfolder are plain text - open any
.ps1 in Notepad and read every line. Search for "Invoke-Web" and you
will find EXACTLY ONE result: the opt-in LOLDrivers fetch in
alibi-engine\scanner\forensic-common.ps1. Search for
"Remove-Item" or "Set-Item" and you'll find none in the scan path.
The whole story is disclosed in every report's header and in:

  alibi-engine\scanner\alibi-safety-card.html
