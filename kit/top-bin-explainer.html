What is _top.bin?

A picture-first explanation of one file the scanner cares about — why it almost never shows up on innocent computers, and what it actually does when it does.

The 30-second version

The whole setup, in one picture

A cheater builds _top.bin on their own PC. They flash it onto an FPGA card — a small custom circuit board. The card plugs into a gaming PC's motherboard. A cable runs back to the cheater's PC. The card silently reads what the game is doing and sends it to the cheater's monitor.

Step 1 · What kind of file is it?

It's not like the other files on your computer

song.mp3
opens in a music app, plays sound
letter.docx
opens in Word, shows text
photo.jpg
opens in Photos, shows a picture
_top.bin
cannot be opened. cannot be played. only one thing it's for.

The first three files do something for you when you double-click them. The last one doesn't open in anything. It exists for one purpose only: to be burned onto a piece of hardware. Think of it less like a document and more like a Lego instruction manual — useless on its own, dangerous when followed.

Step 2 · How does it get made?

You can't download one. You have to build it.

Producing a _top.bin means: (A) downloading specialized source code, (B) installing 60+ GB of professional engineering software, (C) running a multi-hour compile, then (D) buying a custom circuit board to flash it onto. This is not something that happens by accident, or while browsing the web, or by clicking the wrong link.

Step 3 · What the hardware does once it's installed

It reads the game's memory while no one's looking

Anti-cheat software (Ricochet, EAC, BattlEye) lives inside the CPU and watches what other software does. But the DMA card doesn't run as software — it's a separate device that reads memory directly, the same way your graphics card does. The anti-cheat literally cannot see the card, the same way a security guard watching the front door can't see someone climbing in the back window.

Step 4 · What this gives the cheater

The game becomes transparent

A normal player sees

Walls block the view. You don't know where the enemy is. Reaction time, sound, and skill decide who wins.

A DMA cheater sees

Walls are transparent. Every enemy has a glowing box around them with health, distance, and direction. The crosshair gets pulled to targets automatically.

The cheater isn't a better player. The cheater has perfect information — they know where every enemy is, how much health they have, and what they're doing, at all times, regardless of walls or line of sight. It is the difference between playing chess and playing chess while seeing the opponent's next three moves.

Step 5 · Could it be innocent?

Sometimes — but only for certain people

Hardware engineer
"I write code for the chips in medical devices. _top.bin is what my job outputs every day."
Innocent
EE student
"I'm taking a digital logic course. The university uses Xilinx tools and we build little projects all semester."
Innocent
Gamer with one in Downloads
"I play Call of Duty. There's a _top.bin in my Downloads folder inside something called pcileech-squirrel."
Not innocent

The same file in different hands means different things. Context is everything. A hardware engineer's work folder full of _top.bin files is normal. The same file in a gamer's Downloads folder, inside a folder named after a known DMA-cheat board, is not.

What the scan can and can't tell you

The honest limits

Finding one is strong evidence. Not finding one means very little.

If the scan finds a _top.bin on a gamer's computer, the most reasonable conclusion is that someone here was building hardware to cheat. It is still possible to imagine an innocent explanation (an unusual hobbyist, a borrowed PC) — but those explanations are rare and easy to verify.

The reverse is not true. The absence of _top.bin doesn't prove anything. DMA cheats are designed so the gaming PC and the building PC can be separate computers. A clean scan on the gaming PC says nothing about whether a development PC exists somewhere else.

This explainer covers one specific file. The full forensic scan looks at thirteen different categories of evidence and scores them by confidence. _top.bin is one of the strongest single signals. It is not the only one.
